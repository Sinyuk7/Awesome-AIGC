How to Prompt Nano Banana: Core Framework
The 6 Essential Elements
Every effective Nano Banana Pro prompt includes these components:

1. Subject
Define who or what appears in the image with specific characteristics.

Weak: "a woman"
Strong: "a confident entrepreneur in her early 40s with shoulder-length dark hair"
2. Composition
Specify camera framing, angles, and shot types.

Examples: "close-up shot," "wide-angle view," "low-angle perspective," "overhead composition"
3. Action
Describe what's happening in the scene.

Examples: "reviewing analytics on tablet," "presenting to clients," "preparing ingredients"
4. Setting
Establish the environment and context.

Examples: "modern glass-walled office," "industrial kitchen at golden hour," "minimalist studio backdrop"
5. Style
Define the visual aesthetic and mood.

Examples: "cinematic editorial style," "clean product photography," "lifestyle documentary approach"
6. Technical Specifications (Advanced)
Add camera and lighting details for professional control.

Examples: "shot with 85mm f/1.8 lens," "soft window light from left," "shallow depth of field"

Annotation-Based Editing in LTX
Annotation editing gives you precise control over specific image areas without affecting the rest of the composition—one of Nano Banana Pro's most powerful features inside LTX.

How Annotation Works
In the Gen Space, you can mark specific areas of your image and provide targeted instructions for those regions only. The model understands spatial relationships and applies edits with surgical precision.

Best Use Cases for Annotations
Selective object replacement: Change one element while preserving everything else
Localized lighting adjustments: Modify shadows or highlights in specific areas
Background transformations: Swap environments without re-generating the subject
Detail refinement: Enhance texture, color, or clarity in marked regions
Compositional adjustments: Move, scale, or reframe specific elements
Annotation Prompt Examples
Background Replacement: "[Mark background area] Replace this background with a minimalist concrete wall, maintain all lighting and shadows on the subject"

Object Addition: "[Mark empty desk area] Add a laptop and coffee cup in this space, matching the existing lighting and perspective"

Selective Color Adjustment: "[Mark product area] Change only this product to navy blue while keeping all other colors and textures identical"

Lighting Modification: "[Mark left side of face] Add subtle rim lighting from this direction, creating soft edge definition without affecting overall exposure"

Annotation-based editing reduces iteration cycles and maintains continuity—critical for professional workflows where consistency matters.

Photography & Technical Language
Lens & Focal Length
Different focal lengths create distinct visual perspectives:

Wide-angle (18-35mm): Expansive scenes, environmental context, dynamic perspective
Standard (50mm): Natural perspective matching human vision, versatile for most scenes
Portrait (85-135mm): Flattering compression, subject isolation, professional headshots
In prompts: "shot with 85mm lens" or "wide-angle 24mm view"

Aperture & Depth of Field
Control what's in focus to direct viewer attention:

Shallow (f/1.4-f/2.8): Subject isolation, blurred background, cinematic look
Medium (f/4-f/5.6): Balanced focus for environmental context
Deep (f/8-f/16): Everything sharp, ideal for product details or landscapes
In prompts: "shallow depth of field at f/1.8" or "deep focus keeping entire scene sharp"

Lighting Terminology
Golden hour: Warm, soft light at sunrise or sunset
High-key: Bright, minimal shadows, optimistic mood
Low-key: Dramatic shadows, moody atmosphere
Rim lighting: Backlight creating edge glow and separation
Three-point lighting: Professional studio setup with key, fill, and rim
Example: "Golden hour rim lighting creating warm edge glow with soft shadows"

Shot Types & Angles
Close-up (CU): Face or product detail
Medium shot (MS): Waist up, conversational framing
Wide shot (WS): Full scene showing environment
Low angle: Camera below subject (power, authority)
High angle: Camera above subject (overview, context)
Eye level: Natural, neutral perspective

Common Nano Banana Pro Prompt Challenges & Solutions
Issue: Text Rendering Inconsistencies
Solutions:

Limit to 3-5 words maximum
Use quotation marks for exact spelling
Specify font characteristics clearly
For critical applications, add text in post-production
Issue: Composition Not Matching Vision
Solutions:

Explicitly state shot type (close-up, wide, medium)
Include camera angle (low, high, eye-level)
Describe spatial relationships clearly
Use photography terminology (rule of thirds, centered, off-center)
Issue: Lighting Doesn't Match Expectation
Solutions:

Specify light direction (from left, backlit, overhead)
Define light quality (soft, hard, dramatic, natural)
Include time of day references
Mention specific setups (three-point, rim lighting, golden hour)
Issue: Inconsistent Results Across Iterations
Solutions:

Use the refinement approach, not complete re-generation
Save successful results as Elements for reference
Be more specific with technical details
Include style references in your prompt
