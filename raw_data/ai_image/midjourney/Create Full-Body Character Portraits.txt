# Create Full-Body Character Portraits

<aside>
<img src="/icons/magic-wand_gray.svg" alt="/icons/magic-wand_gray.svg" width="40px" />

### Overview (TL;DR)

To get all of your subject (character, person, animal) within the image frame (head-to-toe), follow this checklist:

- Start your prompt with the artistic medium/style.
- Introduce your subject as a **full-length** *(do not say: Full Body, Shot, Portrait)*
- Describe the **top** (head, hair, hat) + the **bottom** (feet, paws, shoes), including what the subject is standing on. As InfoGuru says, the **foot-wear** and the **foot-where**!
- Use as Aspect Ratio that is tall enough to include the whole subject.

*Example: Photograph of a full-length female elf wearing a flower in her hair, walking on a forest path with bare feet. --ar 2:3*

## How do I get my whole character in the image, from head to toe?

</aside>

To get an image that includes **all** of the person or character from head to toe, and NOT a head-and-shoulders portrait, do all of the things in this checklist:

### **Full-Length checklist**

1. **Start your prompt with the artistic medium/style (painting, photograph, illustration, etc):**
    
    ✅  `Illustration of …`
    
    ✅  `Photograph of …`
    
2. **Introduce your subject as a full-length (person, character, animal)**:
    
    ✅  `… a full-length female elf …`
    
    ❌  *do not say* Full Body - the moderation filter can be sensitive to this word/phrasing
    
    ❌  *do not say* Shot - has more than one meaning, and although it’s used for photos it’s best to be clear about the style you actually want.
    
    ❌  *do not say* Portrait - usually cropped at waist or shoulders.
    
3. **Anchor parts of the person/character that tend to fall out of frame**, which works because Midjourney strives to obey us. If you describe it, Midjourney will try to depict it:
    - Mention the feet/footwear (paws and tail if it’s an animal). If you mention `running shoes` or `bare feet` or `big orange paws`, Midjourney is obligated to show them if it wants to obey your prompt.
    - Mention the hair/hat (or ears if an animal).
4. **Describe the scene:** the background and context that help Midjourney create the full environment the person or character is in, including elements above and below the character.
    - Mention the ground/floor like `stands on wooden floorboards` or `stands in the mud`. You can also this with by describing poses in a setting, like `patrolling the forest`, or `walking down the sidewalk`, or `jogging through the park`, etc.
    - Mention something above them like `blue sky above` or `pine branches above`
5. **Use an Aspect Ratio that is tall enough to fit the standing person/character** (parameter --ar).

Still having trouble?

- Keep parameters at their default settings until your prompt is working and consistently giving you full-length views (stylize, chaos, weird, exp).
- Consider taking advantage of the "slope of influence" a.k.a. word sequence. Words at the front of the prompt are more influential than words at the end of the prompt. So starting with full-length and describing the person/character’s extremities at the front of the prompt may be beneficial.

### Example 1

```
Illustration in detailed comic style of a full-length female elf with blue hair wandering in a sunny forest, with her bare feet walking on lush green moss. Above her head is a magical bluebird. --ar 2:3 --v 7
```
